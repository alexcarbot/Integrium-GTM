DMS Integration Enhancements and Smarter Lead Scoring

IP
Integrium Product 6 min read

A Deeper Connection to Your Dealership’s Core Systems

Today we’re shipping the most significant integration update since Integrium’s launch. This release deepens our connections to CDK Global and Reynolds & Reynolds — the two dealer management systems that power the vast majority of franchise dealerships across North America — while introducing an entirely new intent-based lead scoring engine that fundamentally changes how your team prioritizes opportunities.

These aren’t incremental polish. They represent a shift in how Integrium interacts with your operational data, moving from periodic syncs to real-time, bidirectional data flow that makes every AI conversation smarter, every lead score more accurate, and every handoff to your sales team more complete.

Release highlights at a glance:

  • Real-time bidirectional sync with CDK Global and Reynolds & Reynolds
  • Live inventory data now powers every AI customer conversation
  • New intent-based lead scoring engine with 12 behavioral signals
  • Automated workflow triggers based on lead score thresholds
  • Average DMS data latency reduced from 15 minutes to under 30 seconds

CDK Global Integration: What’s New

Our CDK Global integration has been rebuilt from the ground up using CDK’s latest Fortellis API platform. The previous integration relied on scheduled file exports and periodic polling — a pattern that introduced data staleness and occasional sync conflicts. The new architecture establishes a persistent, event-driven connection that pushes changes in real time as they happen in the DMS.

Real-Time Inventory Sync

When a vehicle sells, arrives on the lot, goes into reconditioning, or has a price adjustment in CDK, Integrium reflects that change within seconds. This means the AI never tells a customer about a vehicle that sold yesterday morning or quotes a price that was updated an hour ago. The practical impact on conversation quality is substantial — customers receive accurate information on the first interaction, reducing friction and building trust from the very first message.

Customer Record Enrichment

The integration now pulls richer customer data from CDK, including service history summaries, previous purchase records, and open repair orders. When the AI engages a returning customer, it has context that allows for more relevant conversations — acknowledging their history with the dealership, referencing their current vehicle, and personalizing recommendations based on their service patterns and ownership timeline.

Activity Write-Back

Every AI conversation, appointment set, and lead status change in Integrium is now written back to CDK in real time. Your sales managers don’t need to check a separate dashboard. All activity appears in the CRM they already use, with full conversation transcripts linked to the customer record. This eliminates the “dual system” problem that has plagued dealership technology adoption for years.

Reynolds & Reynolds Integration: What’s New

The Reynolds & Reynolds integration follows a similar philosophy of deep, real-time connectivity, adapted to the specific architecture and data models of the Reynolds platform.

ERA and POWER DMS Support

The updated integration supports both the ERA and POWER platforms, covering the full range of Reynolds deployments. Configuration is handled during onboarding with no ongoing IT maintenance required from the dealership. We worked directly with Reynolds & Reynolds during development to ensure our data access patterns align with their performance and security requirements.

Desking and F&I Data Access

A key enhancement for Reynolds dealers is the ability for Integrium to reference desking parameters and F&I product availability when engaging with customers. The AI can provide more informed responses to payment and financing questions, setting appropriate expectations before the customer arrives at the dealership. This pre-qualification reduces time spent in the F&I office and improves customer satisfaction scores.

Unified Reporting

Integrium’s reporting engine now pulls directly from Reynolds data to create unified performance views that combine AI engagement metrics with traditional DMS metrics. Dealers can see a single dashboard that shows the full funnel: from lead submission through AI engagement, appointment setting, showroom visit, and closed deal — all without exporting data between systems.

“The DMS is the heartbeat of every dealership. When your AI platform can read and write to it in real time, you eliminate an entire category of operational friction. That’s what this release is about — making the integration layer invisible so dealers can focus on selling cars.”
— Integrium Product Team

Introducing Intent-Based Lead Scoring

Alongside the DMS integration upgrades, this release introduces a fundamentally new approach to lead scoring. Traditional lead scoring in automotive relies heavily on source attribution and basic demographic data — essentially asking “where did this lead come from?” and assigning a static score based on historical conversion rates for that source.

Intent-based scoring goes further. It analyzes behavioral signals from the customer’s actual interactions to dynamically assess how ready they are to buy — right now, today.

How It Works

The scoring engine evaluates 12 distinct behavioral signals across two categories: pre-engagement signals (what the customer did before submitting a lead) and conversation signals (what the customer says and does during the AI interaction).

Pre-engagement signals include:

  • VDP view depth and frequency: A customer who viewed 15 vehicle detail pages over three days shows different intent than one who clicked a single ad.
  • Trade-in tool usage: Customers who used an online trade-in estimator are signaling active readiness to transact.
  • Payment calculator engagement: Using financing tools indicates the customer is moving from browsing to budgeting.
  • Return visit patterns: Multiple sessions across different days suggest sustained interest rather than casual browsing.
  • Time spent on specific inventory: Dwelling on a single vehicle’s page for extended periods correlates with high purchase intent.

Conversation signals include:

  • Specificity of vehicle questions: Asking about a specific trim, color, or package versus general inquiries.
  • Timeline language: Phrases like “this weekend,” “as soon as possible,” or “before the end of the month” are strong buying signals.
  • Trade-in mentions: Proactively bringing up a current vehicle indicates active replacement intent.
  • Financing questions: Asking about rates, terms, or down payment requirements signals a customer who is mentally committed and working through logistics.
  • Objection patterns: The types of objections raised (price, availability, timing) reveal where the customer sits in their decision process.
  • Response velocity: How quickly the customer replies during the AI conversation. Faster responses indicate higher engagement and urgency.
  • Appointment willingness: Readiness to commit to a specific date and time versus vague “sometime next week” responses.

Each signal carries a weighted score that adjusts dynamically based on the combination of signals present. A customer who used the trade-in tool, viewed a specific vehicle eight times, and responds to the AI within 30 seconds asking about availability “this Saturday” receives a fundamentally different score than a customer who submitted a generic form from a third-party site and hasn’t responded to outreach.

What Dealers See

Lead scores are displayed as a simple 0–100 scale in both the Integrium dashboard and your CRM (via the DMS write-back). Scores update in real time as new signals are detected. A lead that starts at 45 after initial submission might jump to 82 after a high-intent AI conversation — and your team sees that change immediately.

Scores are also accompanied by a plain-language summary explaining the primary intent signals detected. Instead of just seeing a number, your BDC agent sees context like: “High intent: customer viewed 2024 Camry XSE 6 times, used trade-in estimator, asked about Saturday availability, responds within 30 seconds.”

Workflow Improvements

With richer DMS data and smarter lead scoring, we’ve also shipped a set of workflow automation improvements that connect these capabilities into actionable processes:

  • Score-based routing: Leads above a configurable threshold (default: 75) are automatically flagged for immediate human follow-up and routed to your top closers. Leads below 40 continue in AI nurture sequences.
  • Inventory-aware assignment: When a customer expresses interest in a specific vehicle, the lead is automatically routed to the salesperson assigned to that inventory segment or manufacturer brand.
  • Escalation triggers: If a high-scoring lead hasn’t received human follow-up within 15 minutes, an escalation notification is sent to the sales manager with the full conversation context.
  • Service-to-sales handoff: When a customer with an aging vehicle (identified via DMS service data) engages with the AI, the conversation can intelligently pivot to discuss replacement options, creating a new sales opportunity from your existing service lane traffic.

What’s Coming Next

This release lays the foundation for several capabilities on our near-term roadmap. Here’s a preview of what’s in development:

  1. Predictive inventory matching: Using the combination of intent signals and real-time inventory data to proactively suggest alternative vehicles when a customer’s first choice is unavailable.
  2. Multi-rooftop lead optimization: For dealer groups, intelligently routing leads across rooftops based on inventory availability, geographic proximity, and historical conversion performance at each location.
  3. Expanded DMS support: Dealertrack and Auto/Mate integrations are in active development, with expected availability in Q2 2026.
  4. Service appointment AI: Extending the conversational AI platform to handle inbound service appointment requests with the same speed and intelligence currently applied to sales leads.

Availability: The CDK Global and Reynolds & Reynolds integration enhancements are available now for all Integrium customers on the Enterprise plan. Intent-based lead scoring is rolling out to all plans over the next two weeks. If you have questions about your deployment, reach out to your customer success manager or contact our team.
