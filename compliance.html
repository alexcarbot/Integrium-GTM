Regulatory Landscape

The regulations that matter for your dealership

AI-powered customer communication is subject to a complex web of federal, provincial, and state-level regulations. Here's what you need to know.

United States

TCPA

The Telephone Consumer Protection Act regulates automated calls, texts, and faxes. For dealerships using AI to communicate with customers, TCPA requires explicit prior consent before sending automated messages, and violations carry penalties of $500-$1,500 per message.

  • Prior express written consent required for marketing texts
  • Immediate opt-out processing mandated
  • Class-action exposure for systematic violations
Canada

CASL

Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation governs all commercial electronic messages. It distinguishes between express and implied consent, each with different validity periods and requirements. Penalties can reach $10 million per violation for organizations.

  • Express consent has no expiry; implied consent expires after 2 years
  • Every message must include sender identification
  • Functional unsubscribe mechanism required
State Level

State Privacy Laws

A growing patchwork of state-level privacy regulations adds complexity. California's CCPA/CPRA, Virginia's VCDPA, Colorado's CPA, and others impose varying requirements for data collection, consent, and consumer rights in automated communications.

  • Consumer right to know what data is collected
  • Right to delete and opt out of data sales
  • Varying enforcement timelines across states
Under the Hood

How compliance works in Integrium

Our compliance engine operates in real time across every customer interaction, not as a periodic batch process.

1

Consent Capture

Every interaction begins with a consent verification. The system checks consent status, type (express/implied), channel authorization, and expiry dates before any outbound communication is sent.

2

Real-Time Monitoring

As conversations flow, the compliance engine monitors for opt-out signals, regulatory triggers, and consent boundary conditions. Opt-out requests are processed within seconds — not hours.

3

Audit Logging

Every message, consent event, and opt-out request is recorded in an immutable audit log. Logs are timestamped, searchable, and exportable for legal review or regulatory response.

4

Reporting

Compliance dashboards provide real-time visibility into consent coverage, opt-out rates, and regulatory risk indicators. Automated alerts flag potential issues before they become problems.
